Using occupational health to help an employee 

An employer may have concerns about an employee because:

  • there's been a change in how the employee is performing at work
  • the employee has been off sick more often than normal 
  • the employee is long-term sick (off work sick for more than 4 weeks)

If an employer has concerns, they can ask the employee if they'll agree to an occupational health assessment. 

The employee does not have to agree to it, but it's a good idea as it can help them:

  • get back to work 
  • get any support they might need

What happens in an occupational health assessment 

During the assessment, an occupational health adviser will talk to the employee. The adviser could be a doctor, nurse, or other occupational health professional. 

They will ask the employee about:

  • their health and any treatment they're having
  • their usual work activities
  • their general lifestyle and activities outside work 

The conversation is confidential. The adviser will not share anything without the employee's consent.  

What happens after the assessment

The occupational health adviser will send a report to the employee. They will check with the employee if they can show the report to their employer. 

The report will contain advice and recommendations, which could include:

  • adjustments to the employee's workspace, such as a bigger screen or ergonomic chair
  • a phased return to work or different working hours
  • a referral to other services, like physiotherapy

The employer and employee should then decide the best course of action.
