How the workplace health assessment discount scheme works 

1: Check if your business is eligible and get a discount code.

2: Search the list of providers and book an assessment.

3: Give the provider your discount code.

4: After the assessment, receive advice about free or funded onward support for your employee.

5: Pay the provider. They will send you an invoice.

You can use this scheme for all your employees.

An employee can have as many assessments as they like, but you will need to get a new code each time.

Discount codes do not expire.

What an assessment will cost 

An assessment will cost between £20 and £80 after the 80% discount.

Why the cost of assessments differ 

The price can be different depending on:

  • whether the assessment includes travel costs
  • the complexity of the case
  • the type of health care professional that does the assessment

Examples

Sam has a video call assessment with a nurse. It costs £150, so his employer pays £30.

Emma has an video call with an occupational therapist. It costs £250, so her employer pays £50.

What happens if there are travel costs

Most assessments will be by video call, but some may need to be in person. This can involve extra costs due to travel.

You can claim for travel costs related to an assessment. The 80% discount applies up to a full cost of £400. You will need to pay any remaining costs above £400.

Examples

Joe has an in-person assessment with an occupational therapist. It costs £200 and the travel costs £40, bringing the total to £240. His employer pays £48.

Kat has an in-person assessment with a doctor. The assessment costs £370 and the travel costs £50, bringing the total to £420. Her employer pays £100 in total.

If an employee needs to cancel an assessment

Cancellation of an assessment might result in a fee.

You need to check with the workplace health provider what their cancellation policy is.

If an employee does not attend an assessment

You will have to pay for the full cost of an assessment if your employee does not attend.

Reassuring and reminding your employee about the assessment process means they are more likely to attend.
