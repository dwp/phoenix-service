Get expert health support at work 

Occupational health services can help you manage your health and wellbeing at work. 

If something to do with your health is affecting you at work, you may find it useful to speak to an occupational health adviser. 

You can ask your employer if they'll consider referring you for an assessment. Or your employer may ask if you'll agree to one. 

You do not have to agree, but an assessment can help you get any support you might need to:

  • stay in work
  • get back to work if you've been off sick 

What happens in an occupational health assessment

Occupational health advisers provide impartial advice to help you and your employer. They could be doctors, nurses, or other health professionals.

During the assessment, they'll talk to you about: 

  • your health and any treatment you're having
  • your usual work activities
  • your general lifestyle and activities outside work 

What happens after an assessment

The conversation between you and the adviser is confidential. They will not share anything without your consent.  

The adviser will prepare a report. They'll check with you if they can show it to your employer. If you agree, you can also choose whether you want to see the report first, or at the same time as your employer. 

The report will contain advice and recommendations, which could include: 

  • adjustments to your workspace, such as a bigger screen or ergonomic chair
  • a phased return to work or different working hours
  • a referral to other services, like physiotherapy

You and your employer should decide together the best course of action.  

How to get an assessment

Search the list to find an occupational health provider to do an assessment for you. You can filter by specialism, location and cost.

Once you have found a provider you should discuss the details with your employer. 

Your employer will be responsible for making first contact with the provider. When they do this, the provider will ask about:

  • the details of your situation
  • who they recommend to do your assessment - a doctor, nurse or other health professional
  • whether they need to see you employee in person - this could be to assess your workspace

Your employer will pay for the assessment. The price will depend on which occupational health adviser carries out the assessment.
