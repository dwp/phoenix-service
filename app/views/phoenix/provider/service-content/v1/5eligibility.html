Eligibility   

There are different eligibility requirements, depending on whether your organisation is SEQOHS (Safe, Effective Quality Occupational Health Service) accredited or not. 

In general, to be accepted, we will need to be satisfied that you: 

  • have the required professional industry qualifications and governance procedures and policies 
  • can deliver a quality, professional assessment 

Evidence and declarations required     

For all organisations 

Any occupational health and vocational rehabilitation providers wanting to join the scheme will need to send us:

  • declarations about the service they provide
  • an example of a template or redacted report

The declarations are that:

  • there will be a conversation or interaction with the employer before the assessment to understand the context of their business 
  • they will have a conversation with the employee during the assessment to discuss what will be included in the report and/or the employee will be given the option to see the report prior to sending it to their employer  
  • the person undertaking assessments is, or has oversight from:  
    • - an occupational health physician   
    • - an occupational health nurse
    • - an occupational therapist with experience in workplace settings, vocational rehabilitation or return to work  
    • - a physiotherapist with experience in workplace settings, vocational rehabilitation, or return to work  
    • - a psychologist with experience in workplace settings, vocational rehabilitation, or return to work  
  • the maximum cost for an assessment through this service will be £400   

The example of a template or redacted report must show:

  • a remote or in-person assessment of an employee
  • the writing of a comprehensive report, to include:
    • - the answering of any employer or management concerns  
    • - the employee’s capacity for employment  
    • - advice on the current functional ability of employee  
    • - objective and impartial advice  
    • - onward referrals (if required)  
    • - an opportunity for follow-up discussion  
    • - a biopsychosocial approach  

If your organisation is SEQOHS accredited 

You will need to tell us your organisation name that is registered with SEQOHS. 

If your organisation is not SEQOHS accredited 

You will need to send us evidence that you meet our standards concerning: 

  • clinical governance procedures 
  • complaints procedures and feedback mechanisms 
  • data management policies 
  • referral pathways 

Clinical governance procedures 

You will need to send us: 

  • evidence that you are, or that you employ, at least one health professional (or oversee at least one associate), who has a qualification or equivalent experience in occupational health. The qualification and experience expectations, and evidence required for each professional are listed below.
    • Occupational health physician - We will need to see a General Medical Council number. We will also need evidence of any one of the following:
      • - Diploma in Occupational Medicine (DOccMed)  
      • - Associates of the Faculty of Occupational Medicine (AFOM)  
      • - Members of the Faculty of Occupational Medicine (MFOM)
      • - any other occupational medicine qualification
    • If you cannot provide evidence of any qualifications, you can tell us about any relevant workplace health experience you, your employee or associate may have.
    • Occupational health nurse - We will need to see a Nursing and Midwifery Council number. We will also need evidence of either of the following:
      • - Certificate, diploma or degree which may be recorded on Part 3 of the NMC register Specialist Community Public Health Nursing (SCPHN)  
      • - any other occupational health qualification
    • If you cannot provide evidence of any qualifications, you can tell us about any relevant workplace health experience you, your employee or associate may have.
    • Occupational therapist with experience in workplace settings, vocational rehabilitation, or return to work. - We will need to see a Health and Care Professions Council number. We will also need evidence of either of the following:
      • - Degree (BSc or MSc) in Occupational Therapy  
      • - any other occupational health qualification
    • If you cannot provide evidence of any qualifications, you can tell us about any relevant workplace health experience you, your employee or associate may have.
    • Physiotherapist with experience in workplace settings, vocational rehabilitation, or return to work. - We will need to see a Health and Care Professions Council number. We will also need evidence of one of the following:
      • - Degree (BSc or MSc) in Physiotherapy
      • - Qualifications in Occupational Health, for example Association of Chartered Physiotherapists in Occupational Health and Ergonomics (ACPOHE)
      • - any other occupational health qualification
    • If you cannot provide evidence of any qualifications, you can tell us about any relevant workplace health experience you, your employee or associate may have.
    • Psychologist with experience in workplace settings/vocational rehabilitation/return to work. - We will need to see a Health and Care Professions Council number. We will also need evidence of either of the following:
      • - Degree (BSc, MSc or Doctorate) accredited by the British Psychological Society
      • - any other occupational health qualification
    • If you cannot provide evidence of any qualifications, you can tell us about any relevant workplace health experience you, your employee or associate may have.
  • evidence that all clinical staff are professionally indemnified
  • evidence of clinical governance and compliance with evidence-based and consensus-based guidelines, as well as with professional legal requirements, for example examples of protocols, or policies including version control mechanism  
  • evidence of undertaking systematic audit of clinical practice, for example annual audit plans, or outputs from the most recent report  

Complaints procedures and feedback mechanisms 

You will need show that: 

  • you have a complaints procedure  
  • you proactively request feedback 
  • you have a whistle-blowing policy or equivalent process which allows employees to raise concerns about how services are delivered 

You can either send copies of the relevant documents or explain how your organisation manages complaints and feedback.

Data management policies 

You will need to send a copy of:

  • your privacy policy
  • evidence of registration with the Information Commissioner’s Office, for example a certificate from ICO 
  • your record management policy, including safe transportation, access, storage, retention (GDPR) and disposal of records

Referral pathways

You will need to show that you have: 

  • clearly documented incoming and on-going referral procedures, including processes for referrals to your service, case management and any onward referral for further investigation 
  • have access to an identified occupational physician, listed on the GMC specialist register, for the escalation of cases, for example a signed agreement which includes the arrangement where a consultation would be made or evidence of having previously accessed an OH physician for nurse-led services.
